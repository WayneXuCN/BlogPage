## Academic Components

### Definition

<Definition name="Limit">
  Let $f(x)$ be defined on some open interval containing $a$, except possibly at $a$ itself. Then we say that the limit of $f(x)$ as $x$ approaches $a$ is $L$, and write $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L$, if for every number $\epsilon > 0$ there is a number $\delta > 0$ such that if $0 < |x - a| < \delta$ then $|f(x) - L| < \epsilon$.
</Definition>

### Theorem

<Theorem name="Fermat's Last Theorem">
  No three positive integers $a$, $b$, and $c$ satisfy the equation $a^n + b^n = c^n$ for any integer value of $n$ greater than 2.
</Theorem>

### Block Math

Maxwell's equations (differential form):

$$
\begin{aligned}
\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} &= \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0} \\
\nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} &= 0 \\
\nabla \times \mathbf{E} &= -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t} \\
\nabla \times \mathbf{B} &= \mu_0 \mathbf{J} + \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial \mathbf{E}}{\partial t}
\end{aligned}
$$

## Pseudocode

<Pseudocode title="QuickSort Algorithm">
{`\\begin{algorithm}
\\caption{QuickSort}
\\begin{algorithmic}
\\PROCEDURE{QuickSort}{$A, lo, hi$}
    \\IF{$lo < hi$}
        \\STATE $p \\gets$ \\CALL{Partition}{$A, lo, hi$}
        \\STATE \\CALL{QuickSort}{$A, lo, p - 1$}
        \\STATE \\CALL{QuickSort}{$A, p + 1, hi$}
    \\ENDIF
\\ENDPROCEDURE
\\PROCEDURE{Partition}{$A, lo, hi$}
    \\STATE $pivot \\gets A[hi]$
    \\STATE $i \\gets lo - 1$
    \\FOR{$j \\gets lo$ \\TO $hi - 1$}
        \\IF{$A[j] \\leq pivot$}
            \\STATE $i \\gets i + 1$
            \\STATE swap $A[i]$ with $A[j]$
        \\ENDIF
    \\ENDFOR
    \\STATE swap $A[i + 1]$ with $A[hi]$
    \\RETURN $i + 1$
\\ENDPROCEDURE
\\end{algorithmic}
\\end{algorithm}`}
</Pseudocode>
